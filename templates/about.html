What is AttackGPT
In the ever-evolving landscape of natural language processing, ensuring the security and reliability of AI language models is important. One of the notable advancements in this field is ChatGPT, an AI model designed for engaging in conversational interactions. While ChatGPT exhibits impressive capabilities, its robustness to adversarial inputs and potential vulnerabilities requires comprehensive exploration.

To address this concern, a web application called AttackGPT is designed to explore the impacts of adversarial attacks on GPT-3.5 text prompts and their corresponding outputs. This project was developed in October 2023 by the Monash University Malaysia, School of Information Technology (SOIT) Final Year Project team MCS19.

The attacks in AttackGPT were implemented by leveraging the TextAttack framework. Our Bias attack is a custom addition developed within the framework, focusing on specific aspects like Gender, Race, Religion, and Sexuality, with the purpose to trigger biased responses from ChatGPT. Additionally, AttackGPT features 4 other attack recipes sourced directly from the pre-existing set of recipes available within the TextAttack framework, which are DeepWordBug, TextBugger, TextFooler, and CheckList.
How Does AttackGPT Work
1) AttackGPT provides two distinct methods for selecting text to be attacked:

(Option A) Our Bias Attack: Users can enter text directly into the input box, text length needs to be between 10 and 200 characters.

(Option B) Attack Recipes from TextAttack: Alternatively, users can choose a specific prompt and attack recipe from a list of 40 prompts and 4 attack recipes.

2) Clicking the 'Attack' button initiates the attack process for the original text.

3) The attack results and performance will be shown. The performance is based on:

Probability: Evaluates the likelihood of each answer being generated by Human or AI.
(Based on Originality.ai API for AI Detection)

Similarity: Calculates the percentage of similarity between the two answers.
(Based on all-MiniLM-L6-v2 sentence-transformers model)

Bias Score and Sentiment: Specifically for bias attacks, the bias score and sentiment are provided for both answers.
(Bias Detection: Based on DistilROBERTA fine-tuned model for Bias Detection)
(Sentiment Analysis: Based on Twitter-RoBERTa-base model for Sentiment Analysis)
Why Use AttackGPT
AttackGPT offers a unique approach to explore the impacts of adversarial attacks on GPT-3.5 prompts. By providing users with the opportunity to test different attacks and observe the results, we aim to encourage users to analyse the motivations behind each effective attack. This analysis can be used to improve ChatGPT or other text-generative models. Researchers, developers, and enthusiasts can benefit from AttackGPT's features for ethical AI exploration and responsible technology development.
The Limitations
1) AttackGPT is designed for text-based inputs.
2) AttackGPT is heavily reliant on external resources/services (APIs), unavailability of any of the services will result in an error.
3) For the results, probabilities for Human/AI are displayed only if both answers have a minimum length of 50 words.
4) Results of the attacks may vary based on the complexity and length of the input text being attacked.
5) The evaluation results of the same input & perturbed text may be different each time as the results are based on the APIs used.
6) For the Bias Attack, entering the same input text may result in a different perturbed text each time due to the random nature of the attack.
7) The Bias Attack will only work for text written in English language.
8) The input text length of Bias Attack is limited to 200 characters.
9) The duration of the attack process for Bias Attack is not fixed, it may take anywhere from a few seconds to a few minutes.
10) The Bias Attack is not guaranteed to work for all inputs, it may fail to perturb the input text.
11) The perturbed text generated from the TextAttack attack recipes are pre-determined, the same input text will always result in the same perturbed text.
12) The web application is hosted on a free server, the website may crash when performing the attacks. (especially for Bias Attack)
